# Movement: The Anthropomorphic Echo
## Philosophy: Abstractly Human
This movement explores the contradiction of "digital humanity"—the attempt to capture the essence of human imperfection, biological asymmetry, and emotional fragility using the rigid, cold tools of binary logic. It posits that "humanness" in art is found not in the likeness of a face, but in the *failure* of a pattern to remain perfect.

## Mathematical Axioms
1. **Intentional Asymmetry**: No mirror axis is absolute. Every geometric form is skewed by a "humanity constant" (a random offset).
2. **Pulse-Based Oscillation**: Rhythms are not clock-synced; they follow a simulated heartbeat—a slow, breathing swell that affects the opacity and scale of elements.
3. **The Fragility Layer**: Lines are not continuous. They are subjected to "synaptic gaps," where the path breaks momentarily, mimicking the imperfection of memory and biological transmission.

## Visual Manifestation
- Organic, flowing curves that almost form a silhouette but never quite resolve into a recognizable image.
- A "Skin & Neon" palette: soft, warm flesh tones (beige, coral, peach) interrupted by sharp, surgical streaks of digital light (white-blue, magenta).
- A sense of depth that feels like looking through layered vellum or translucent skin.
